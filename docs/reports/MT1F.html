FunctionMetallothionein-1F is a cysteine-rich heavy metal-binding protein involved in zinc/copper homeostasis, heavy metal detoxification, and oxidative stress defense. MT1F is transcriptionally regulated by MTF1 in response to metal exposure. Notably, MT1F is frequently epigenetically silenced by promoter hypermethylation in multiple cancer types (colorectal, gastric, thyroid, breast), where its loss is associated with enhanced cell proliferation and migration. Re-expression of MT1F suppresses tumor growth, suggesting a tumor suppressor function. In thyroid cancer, MT1F promoter methylation is a diagnostic biomarker. Nuclear import occurs during metal stress and is associated with zinc-dependent regulation of transcription factors.
